Proof

Formulas used by the converter:

  • DMS → Decimal degrees: $$\mathrm{DD} = \operatorname{sign}(D)\cdot\bigl(|D| + \tfrac{M}{60} + \tfrac{S}{3600}\bigr)$$
  • Decimal degrees → DMS:
    $$D = \operatorname{sign}(\mathrm{DD})\cdot\lfloor |\mathrm{DD}| \rfloor,$$ $$M = \Bigl\lfloor (|\mathrm{DD}| - \lfloor |\mathrm{DD}| \rfloor)\cdot 60 \Bigr\rfloor,$$ $$S = \Bigl((|\mathrm{DD}| - \lfloor |\mathrm{DD}| \rfloor)\cdot 60 - M\Bigr)\cdot 60$$
  • Degrees ↔ Radians: $$\mathrm{rad} = \mathrm{deg}\cdot\frac{\pi}{180}, \qquad \mathrm{deg} = \mathrm{rad}\cdot\frac{180}{\pi}$$

Notes: minutes and seconds are non-negative; the sign of the angle is carried on the degrees component.
